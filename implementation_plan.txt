
Robot Learning Implementation Plan - Om, Will, and Ayuj

We establish a baseline by training a standard imitation learning with a Diffusion or flow based matching policy on expert datasets within RoboSuite, ensuring we have a high-fidelity performance metric before introducing complexity. Once the baseline is established, we will generate negative data by “rolling out” this original policy to capture failure cases, such as collisions or task incompletion, which will then be integrated into a Contrastive Flow Matching training pipeline to "push" the policy away from known error states. We will use Bowen’s contrastive flow implementation or Arpit’s diffusion implementation.

Here is an implementation we found via Arpit: https://github.com/Arpitrf/imitation/tree/main

To de-risk this early, we will first validate the contrastive loss on a 2D toy task to ensure the gradient properly separates positive and negative trajectories before moving to high-dimensional robot control. 

We anticipate potential failure modes such as “mode collapse” where the robot becomes overly cautious to avoid failures. The robot could also experience “manifold misalignment” where the negative data is too sparse to provide a useful contrastive signal. If the multi-step inference of flow matching proves too latent for real-time simulation, our primary pivot will be to transition to a one-step "Drifting Model" framework to maintain expressive power while achieving single-pass efficiency.
